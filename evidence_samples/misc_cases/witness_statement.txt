I, James Kowalski, make the following statement of my own free will:

I have worked at DeltaTech Corp for seven years as a Senior Systems
Administrator. Part of my role involves monitoring access logs for our
internal file servers and responding to automated security alerts.

On or around April 30, 2024, I received an automated alert from our
security monitoring system indicating that the account "mvance"
(belonging to Marcus Vance, Head of Operations) had logged into the
corporate file server FILESVR01 at approximately 02:15 AM UTC. This
was unusual because Marcus was recorded as being on approved annual
leave that day, and after-hours logins from the server room VLAN are
flagged by our system.

I reviewed the access logs and found that the session originated from
internal IP address 192.168.1.105. That IP address is not assigned to
Marcus Vance's registered workstation (which is 10.0.0.31). When I
cross-referenced our DHCP lease records, 192.168.1.105 was assigned
to a device with MAC address C4:E9:84:3A:0F:22 — a device that does
not appear in our authorised equipment register.

Over the following days I identified two further similar access events
from the same source IP on May 2nd and May 5th, both between 01:00
and 03:30 AM UTC. Each session accessed the Legal and Finance shared
drives and downloaded files totalling approximately 4.2GB.

On May 2nd I raised my concerns by email to the IT Security team and
separately spoke to Marcus Vance directly. Marcus told me the accesses
were his own, made from home. However, our VPN logs for those dates
show no VPN session associated with his credentials, and a direct
server room connection would require physical badge access.

I subsequently checked the badge access records for the server room.
On April 30th at 02:13 AM, badge access was granted using a card
registered to Derek Henderson, Facilities Manager. I spoke to
Mr Henderson, who confirmed he did not access the building that night
and was unaware his card had been used.

On May 9th, I noticed that approximately 14GB of data had been
compressed into an encrypted archive on a temporary mount path on
FILESVR01. The archive was deleted at 03:47 AM but I was able to
recover metadata showing it had been created at 03:21 AM and accessed
from IP 192.168.1.105.

I have preserved all log files, DHCP records, badge access reports,
and VPN logs on an isolated forensic workstation and have provided
copies to the investigating team.

I believe the evidence strongly suggests that Marcus Vance, or someone
acting with his knowledge, used cloned credentials and an unregistered
device to carry out unauthorised data exfiltration from DeltaTech Corp
systems.

I confirm that this statement is true to the best of my knowledge.

Signed : James Kowalski
